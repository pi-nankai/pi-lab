Chongyi Li (李重仪)

Professor @ Nankai university

Chongyi Li is a Professor with the School of Computer Science, Nankai University, China. Before joining Naikai, he was a Research Assistance Professor from 2021 to 2023 with the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. He was a research fellow with the City University of Hong Kong （supervised by Chair Prof. Sam Kwong） and Nanyang Technological University （supervised by President's Chair Prof. Chen Change Loy） from 2018 to 2021. He received his Ph.D. degree （supervised by Prof. Jichang Guo） from Tianjin University in 2018. He was also a joint training Ph.D. student （supervised by Prof. Fatih Porikli） at Australian National University (ANU), Australia, from 2016 to 2017.

Chunle Guo (郭春乐)

Associate Professor @ Nankai University

Chun-Le Guo is a Associate Professor at the College of Computer Science, Nankai University, China. He received his PhD degree from Tianjin University in 2020. He conducted the Ph.D. research as a Visiting Student with the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science, Queen Mary University of London (QMUL), UK. Then He continued his research as a Research Associate with the Department of Computer Science, City University of Hong Kong (CityU of HK).

Ming-Ming Cheng (程明明)

Professor @ Nankai University

Ming-Ming Cheng is a professor at the College of Computer Science, Nankai University, leading the Media Computing Lab. He received his Ph.D. degree from Tsinghua University in 2012 and then worked with Prof. Philip Torr in Oxford for 2 years. His research interests include computer vision and computer graphics. He has published over 100 papers in leading journals and conferences, such as IEEE TPAMI, ACM TOG, IEEE CVPR, etc. Many of his algorithms have become quite popular in the community, receiving more than 35,000+ paper citations. He received several research awards, including ACM China Rising Star Award, the IBM Global SUR award, etc. He is a senior member of the IEEE and on the editorial boards of the IEEE TPAMI and IEEE TIP.
